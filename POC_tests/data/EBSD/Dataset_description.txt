Attached are two sample datasets which are also on the sharepoint site in t=
he /Software Development/Example datasets folder

https://deliver.ch2m.com/sites/wemdevelopment/SitePages/Hydra%20TSB.aspx

RAINFALL_EXAMPLE_21_YEARS.zip

holds a time series of rainfall data. Not exactly sure of what column is wh=
at, and I can't quite understand the 3rd column, but this data has at least=
 the year and the month and further on some data that could be millimeters =
per day or other time step of rainfall. In the end I think we will need dat=
e, (potential time if sub-daily), value of measurement, unit of measurement=
 and some way to clarify the time step whether in metadata or as another co=
lumn with a repeated value.

This file has 21 years of data. We could conceivable have 100's of years of=
 rainfall, demand, groundwater levels for multiple locations (20 to 100s). =
Most commonly daily, but there could be weekly or monthly. I imagine if the=
 data went sub-daily, there would be much less than 100 years of it. Common=
ly we work with 30 years of data, but more often we will want to work with =
105 years or more.

Phase2b_data_book_v4.7_region_simple.zip

Gives a glimpse at the data we used for our EBSD (Economics of Balancing Su=
pply and Demand) GAMS methodology. There would normally be a few more sheet=
s (and multiple versions of many sheets to hold scenario variations of the =
data.

Basically:

DI,THR, DO, Loss, Outage, SR_baseline, SR_uncertainty  are related to nodes=
 and refer to 4 different annual values for NYAA, DYAA, DYCP and MDO versio=
ns of an annual scenario. You can see 25 years worth of data.

Ex.Transfer are links that have maximum capacities for NYAA, DYAA, DYCP and=
 MDO

Ph2B_Source are nodes to represent potential future supplies and have maxim=
um capacities for NYAA, DYAA, DYCP and MDO

Ph2B_Transfer are links to represent potential future transfer capacity and=
 have maximum capacities for NYAA, DYAA, DYCP and MDO

There are usually Demand Reduction options as well, but I deleted these by =
accident when trimming down the file because of size reasons.

You can see some of the fixed values associated with the Pn2B_Source  and P=
h2B_Transfer options. I had to delete the cost figures for confidentiality =
reasons. This is why we would like to be able to allow encryption of some d=
ata values so some users wouldn't be able to see some confidential attribut=
es.

MutuallyExclusive, MutuallyDependent, Phasing and Capacity give you an idea=
 of how we have organised the network constraints.

We also would like to be able to connect with data stored in the CUAHSI Hyd=
rologic Information System or similar systems. It would be convenient to co=
nnect with or store data in the NetCDF format and of course GIS layers.

http://www.unidata.ucar.edu/software/netcdf/examples/files.html

Please let me know any questions.
